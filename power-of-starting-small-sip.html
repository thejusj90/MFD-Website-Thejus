The Power of Starting Small with SIPs

A small SIP started today usually beats a bigger one planned for 'someday' — here's the maths behind why, and how to think about starting amount.

TJ
Thejus Joseph Updated May 19, 2024 5 min read

Quick answer: For long time horizons, time invested matters more than the starting amount. A small SIP started now typically outgrows a larger SIP started a few years later, purely because of how compounding works — and starting small also builds the habit before income (and the amount) grows.

A common reason people delay starting a SIP is feeling like the amount they can afford right now is "too small to matter." In practice, for anyone with a long runway ahead, starting small today usually beats waiting to start bigger.

Key takeaways

  • Time invested matters more than starting amount for goals 7+ years away.
  • A small SIP started now can outgrow a larger one started even 3-4 years later.
  • Starting small builds the habit and the mandate infrastructure before income grows into a bigger amount.

The maths behind "start small, start now"

Consider two people investing at a 12% expected annual return: Person A starts a ₹2,000/month SIP at age 23. Person B waits until age 27 and starts a ₹4,000/month SIP — double the amount. By age 45, despite investing half as much per month, Person A's extra four years of compounding typically puts their corpus ahead of or very close to Person B's, purely from starting earlier. You can test this directly with your own numbers using the SIP Returns Calculator.

Time invested matters more than the starting amount, for long enough time horizons.

Why the habit matters as much as the number

Starting small also means the mandate, the fund selection, and the monthly rhythm are already in place by the time your income grows. Increasing an existing SIP is a two-minute action; starting one from scratch after years of "planning to start" often keeps getting deprioritized.

💡
Most fund platforms let you set up an automatic annual "step-up" — increasing your SIP amount by a fixed percentage each year in line with expected salary growth, without needing to remember to do it manually.

What to do next

If ₹500 or ₹1,000/month feels like the realistic number for you right now, that's a completely reasonable place to start — the First SIP guide walks through the setup itself.
