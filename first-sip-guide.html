Your First SIP: A Step-by-Step Guide

A practical walkthrough for starting your very first Systematic Investment Plan — from picking an amount to the mandate that debits it automatically.

TJ
Thejus Joseph Updated Jan 14, 2026 6 min read

Quick answer: Starting a SIP takes four steps — complete KYC (one-time, digital), pick a fund category matching your goal and time horizon, choose an amount you can sustain every month without strain, and set up the auto-debit mandate. Most platforms complete this in under 15 minutes.

The idea of starting a SIP can feel like it needs research, comparison, and a big decision upfront. In practice, it's a short, mechanical process — the actual thinking (which category, how much) matters far more than which app or platform you use.

Key takeaways

  • KYC is one-time and fully digital — no branch visits needed.
  • Starting amount matters less than consistency; ₹500/month started today beats ₹5,000/month planned for "someday."
  • The SIP date can be aligned to right after your salary credits, so it never competes with spending.
  • You can pause, increase, or stop a SIP anytime with no penalty from the fund house.

Step 1: Complete your KYC

KYC (Know Your Customer) is a one-time regulatory requirement across all mutual fund investments in India. If you've never invested before, you'll complete Aadhaar-based e-KYC digitally — typically PAN verification, a selfie, and an OTP. This takes a few minutes and doesn't need to be repeated for future investments across fund houses once done via a KRA (KYC Registration Agency).

Step 2: Decide the amount, not the fund, first

Before picking a specific fund, decide what you can invest every month without it straining your regular expenses. A good check: could you sustain this amount even in a month with an unexpected cost? If not, start lower — a SIP that gets skipped or stopped after two months due to cash flow strain does less for you than a smaller one that runs uninterrupted for years.

Consistency beats the "perfect" amount you can't actually sustain.

Step 3: Match the fund category to your time horizon

  • Goal 1–3 years away: debt or conservative hybrid funds — capital preservation matters more than growth here.
  • Goal 3–7 years away: hybrid or large-cap equity funds — moderate growth with contained volatility.
  • Goal 7+ years away: diversified equity funds — time smooths out short-term volatility.

If you're unsure which bracket your goal falls into, the Goal Planning Calculator can help work backward from the goal amount and timeline to a required monthly investment.

💡
Set your SIP debit date for 2–3 days after your salary typically credits. This small timing choice is one of the most reliable ways to keep a SIP running without interruption — the money leaves before it has a chance to get spent elsewhere.

Step 4: Set up the auto-debit mandate

The mandate is a one-time authorization letting the fund house auto-debit your bank account on your chosen date each month — set up via net banking or a UPI Autopay approval, taking a couple of minutes. Once approved, the SIP runs on its own; there's no need to manually invest each month.

What to do next
